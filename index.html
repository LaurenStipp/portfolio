About

Hello!

My name is Lauren Stipp. I'm a senior at Illinois State University majoring in Creative Technology with an emphasis in game design.

In junior high, I decided I wanted a career that had to do something with video games. I started taking computer classes were offered. I hoped to learn how to program and how a computer works.


During my junior year of high school, I signed up to take classes throguh the community college. The program was called ECCA, Early College and Career Academy. The program was run by Parkland Community College in Champign, Illinois.


Taking these classes I earned college credit that I could use to transfer to another college or continue to seek my degree. I graduated high school a semester early and continued my education by becoming a non-degree seeking student at Parkland.


Since starting Illinois State University and changing majors (computer science to Creative Technology), I have been granted many opportunities that I could never dream of accomplishing at my age.
